History
Timeline

On June 19, 1995, Tagbilaran City Science High School became a legal entity. In 1995, there were 97 students in the pioneering batch, divided into the I-Einstein and I-Newton divisions. The modern library and storage spaces were the first two classrooms. Students then wore civilian attire to class because there was no formal school uniform. At JJ's Seafood Village, the first acquaintance party was hosted in a hut made of local materials. The first clubs to be created were the Student Body Organization and the Science, Math, and SSG(currently called the Supreme Student Government).

When Sec.Ricardo Gloria visited the school in 1996, he suggested renaming it "Tagbilaran City Science And Technology High School," but this suggestion was rejected because there was no official government order to do so.

Sir Melchor Daniel Jr. created the school's official uniform and logo in 1997. The Drum and Lyre Corps was started by Sir Melchor Daniel Jr. to highlight the abilities of the students. Carlo Gallego served as the first editor-in-chief of The Equinox, the official school newspaper, which was established in 1999 by Mr. Melchor Daniel Jr. The inaugural graduation ceremony or commencement exercises took place on April 9, 1999, at the Bohol Cultural Center.

The local administration suggested renaming the school Dr. Cecilio Putong National Science High School in 2004. Due to Mr. Melchor Daniel's insistence that the name be preserved because, in his opinion, it is the only high school that bears the name of Tagbilaran City, it was not made possible. The Tagbilaran City Science High School commemorated its tenth year of existence in 2005. It was a fundraising event called "Just Ten" organized by Mrs. Apape and held at the Bohol Cultural Center. Three days and two nights passed during this occurrence.

The school won first place on the National Achievement Test in 2007. As a result, Sec. Jesli Lapus stopped by the school to offer compliments, together with members of the press and the media. The school received air conditioners from city mayor Dan Neri Lim. According to DepEd Order No. 55, s. 2009, Mr. Publio Acain and Mr. Melchor Daniel Jr. exposed the third and fourth year pupils to the new subject of French. 2009.

On July 1, 2011, the school received the "Best Performing School" award from the 45th Tagbilaran City Charter Day. The school gate was renovated in 2014 thanks to funding from the City Government of Tagbilaran and the school FPTA. According to the people who decided on this award, "TCSHS got spectacular points for its school head governance, internal stakeholders, external stakeholders, school resources, School Involvement Plan (SIP) Integration, School Performance Accountability, and students' performance in the division, regional, and national tests."
